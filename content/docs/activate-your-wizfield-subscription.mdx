WizField subscription activation depends on Stripe-backed billing synchronization, not the checkout success redirect alone. Verified Stripe webhook events reconcile local billing state before billing is treated as active product truth.

## Use Stripe Checkout for WizField Billing

Stripe is the active billing provider for WizField. Billing uses a provider abstraction boundary, but Stripe is the current provider implementation.

Owner-authenticated checkout exists in the completed engineering truth. Without live Stripe secrets configured, checkout fails safely with `billing_provider_not_configured` instead of pretending billing is configured.

## Wait for Webhook-Confirmed Subscription State

WizField does not activate billing from the success redirect page alone. Stripe is webhook-authoritative for subscription state synchronization.

After checkout, verified Stripe webhook events reconcile local billing state. The billing success page must not be treated as proof that a subscription has already been activated.

## Understand Shared Billing Accounts

Billing authority lives at the Shared Billing Account layer. The `billing_accounts` model is the authoritative payer and subscription layer, while organization billing linkage projects coverage to organizations.

A single Shared Billing Account may cover multiple organizations under one entitlement. Plan enforcement resolves from organization context through the linked Shared Billing Account.

## Know What Remains Owner Activation Scope

The engineering foundation for Stripe-first shared billing is complete, but live Stripe checkout execution and live webhook delivery remain owner activation scope.

Program/public launch approval, production support details, final legal pages, monitoring choices, and production-like rerun evidence are also outside the completed engineering verdict.
